#01: The Four Horsemen

The SaaSpocalypse and the fight for the control plane.

The Four Horsemen of the SaaSpocalypse

We're in the midst of a fundamental shift in how software is built, deployed, and controlled. Four forces are converging to reshape the entire industry:

1. The Unbundling of Platforms

Monolithic platforms are fracturing into specialized components. Companies can no longer force users into their ecosystems—they must compete component-by-component.

The era of the platform winner-take-all is ending.

2. The Explosion of Data

Every application generates exponentially more data than it did five years ago. Companies that can't manage and move data efficiently will lose competitive advantage.

Data is the new infrastructure.

3. The Rise of the Control Plane

As systems become more distributed and complex, the software that orchestrates them—the control plane—becomes more valuable than the applications themselves.

Kubernetes, GitOps, and platforms-as-products are the future.

4. The Commoditization of Compute

As cloud providers compete on price and performance, compute becomes a commodity. The real value shifts to the software that makes compute useful.

Cloud lock-in is collapsing. Multi-cloud is becoming standard.

What This Means

The companies that win in the next era will be those that:

  • Master data movement and transformation
  • Build best-in-class control planes
  • Embrace open standards and multi-cloud
  • Compete on component quality, not platform lock-in

The Fight Ahead

The SaaSpocalypse isn't a crisis—it's an opportunity. The companies brave enough to embrace these four forces will reshape the industry.

The control plane is the future. What will you build with it?
